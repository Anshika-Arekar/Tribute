Kishore Kumar

Here are some significant milestones in Kishore Kumar's life:

  • 4th August 1929 - Kishore Kumar was born as Abhas Kumar Ganguly in Khandwa, Madhya Pradesh, India.
  • 1950s - Kishore Kumar's playback singing career began to flourish, with songs like "Marne Ki Duayen Kyon Mangu" from the film "Ziddi."
  • 1951 - He married Ruma Guha Thakurta, a Bengali actress and singer. They had a son, Amit Kumar.
  • Late 1950s - Early 1960s - Kishore Kumar's acting career gained momentum with successful films like "New Delhi," "Aasha," and "Half Ticket."
  • 1969-1975 - He delivered some of his most iconic songs during this period, collaborating extensively with music directors R.D. Burman, S.D. Burman, and others.
  • 1975 - Kishore Kumar won his first National Film Award for Best Male Playback Singer for the song "Roop Tera Mastana" from the film "Aradhana."
  • 1980s - He continued to dominate the music scene with hits like "Pag Ghungroo Baandh" from "Namak Halaal" and "Hazaar Raahen Mudke Dekheen" from "Thodisi Bewafaii."
  • 13th October 1987 - Kishore Kumar passed away in Mumbai, Maharashtra, India, leaving behind a legacy of timeless music and memorable performances.
  • Posthumous - Kishore Kumar's influence and popularity continued to grow after his death, with his songs being cherished by generations of music lovers. He remains one of the most celebrated and influential figures in the history of Indian cinema and music.
